Welcome to tips!

This explains a little bit about how this application works!

First, choose whatever progression that you would like to have (ex. Major, Minor, Diminished) and then type the chord that you want to experiment with into the text field or click the piano. The result will be the chord names dislpayed on the screen and colored on the piano.

The other options for chord progressions (djfkldajf;j) are not-your-average- kind-of-chords. These chords are more out there and a lot more different! Don't be afraid to experiment :)

Try as many chords as you want! But if you hear something that you like, make sure that you take note of it, as the chords will disappear if you start to use another chord.

For chord inversion, you just move the lowest notes to the other notes in the chord. For example, triads can be inverted by moving the lowest notes up an octave.

A chord progression is a series of chords played in a sequence; the harmonic functions of chords within a key is compared to the tonic of the key, and thus they are denoted with Roman Numerals.

This explains a little bit about how this application works!

First, choose whatever Chord Family that you would like to have (ex. Major, Minor, Diminished) or you could even play the piano with single chords as well. Then to start, click on the piano key that you want to begin with. The result will be the chord names dislpayed on the screen and colored on the piano.


Once a key is clicked, the notes that go along with the chord are displayed on the screen, right above the piano

Different Kinds of chords

For chord inversion, you just move the lowest notes to the other notes in the chord. For example, triads can be inverted by moving the lowest notes up an octave.

A chord progression is a series of chords played in a sequence; the harmonic functions of chords within a key is compared to the tonic of the key, and representing them as their name using the standard note sheet letters.
